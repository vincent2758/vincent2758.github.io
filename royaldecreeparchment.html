The Royal Decree of Founding

Issued Under the Grand Honour of Him the Most, His Exalted Majesty King Voncelotus I — In the Year of the Greatest Reformation in history of Voncedenkwerk (now Dunkirk)

“Let the divided lands of Voncedenkwerk be united not by sword, nor by blood, but by the voice of its people and the wisdom of its crown.”

By the divine grace and sovereign will of His Exalted Majesty King Voncelotus the First, Sovereign of the Dwekrikes Domain, Defender of the Honour of the Crown, and Keeper of the Twaritwo Seal, it is hereby proclaimed before all noble Houses and loyal citizens:

That a Council of the People shall be established, henceforth known as The House of Commons, wherein the voices of the realm’s faithful citizens shall be heard and weighed in just deliberation.

That a Council of the Nobility, henceforth called The House of Lords, shall stand as guardian of heritage, order, and the continuity of the Crown’s wisdom.

That both Houses shall be bound in duty and honour beneath the emblem of the Black Rod, symbol of peace and obedience, wrought from the iron of the weapons once turned against one another — that they may never again be raised in strife.

And that no law shall pass into the realm’s binding decree save through the harmony of both Houses, under the watchful eye of the Royal Crown.

Thus, by the decree and sacred hand of His Exalted Majesty King Voncelotus I, the first stones of the Voncedenkwerk Parliament are laid, in the name of sovereignity, laws, and eternal duty to the Realm.

Thus hath it been signed and sealed within the Supreme Hall of Voncelotus Court, in witness of the Realm and under the grace of His Majesty. signed

“Where wisdom speaks, let pride be silent.”

— Signed and Sealed at the Supreme Hall of Voncelotus Court
Under the Great Banner of the Dwekrikes Monarchy.
VONCEDENKWERK TWARITWO
King Voncelotus I
In commemoration of the Great Conference of Binding and the founding of the dual Houses of Parliament.
